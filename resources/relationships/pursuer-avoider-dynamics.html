Pursuer and Avoider Dynamics

Understanding the dance between reaching out and pulling away, and how to change the pattern together.

🔥
The Pursuer
Moves towards connection

What it looks like

  • Reaches out more when feeling disconnected
  • Wants to talk things through straight away
  • Asks questions, seeks reassurance
  • May follow their partner from room to room
  • Can become critical when needs are not met

What is underneath

  • A deep need to feel important and valued
  • Fear that silence means something is wrong
  • Reaching out is their way of trying to repair
Core fear: “If I stop reaching, we will drift apart.”
➜ ➜
🔄

The more one pursues, the more the other withdraws

Breaking the cycle

Pause and name the pattern together. When you both recognise the dance, you can choose a different step.

“I think we are in our cycle right now. Can we slow down?”

🥶
The Avoider
Moves away to self-protect

What it looks like

  • Needs space when things get intense
  • Goes quiet, shuts down, or leaves the room
  • Prefers to “let things settle” before talking
  • May seem calm on the outside but feels flooded inside
  • Can appear dismissive or uninterested

What is underneath

  • A deep need to feel competent and not overwhelmed
  • Fear that engaging will make things worse
  • Withdrawing is their way of trying to keep the peace
Core fear: “If I engage, I will get it wrong or make it worse.”
